CARBONARA
Spaghetti alla carbonara is a classic Roman pasta dish. You need spaghetti, guanciale or pancetta, eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, black pepper, and salt. Cook the pasta until al dente. Fry the guanciale in a pan until crispy. Whisk together eggs and grated Pecorino Romano with plenty of black pepper. Remove the pan from heat, add the drained pasta, then pour in the egg mixture and toss quickly so the eggs cook from the residual heat without scrambling. Never add cream — authentic carbonara has no cream.

CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIES
To make chocolate chip cookies you need butter, brown sugar, white sugar, eggs, vanilla extract, all-purpose flour, baking soda, salt, and chocolate chips. Cream the butter and sugars together until light and fluffy. Beat in the eggs and vanilla. Mix in the dry ingredients: flour, baking soda, and salt. Fold in the chocolate chips. Chill the dough for at least 30 minutes for thicker cookies. Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 9 to 11 minutes until golden at the edges but still soft in the center.

CHICKEN SOUP
A good chicken soup starts with a whole chicken or bone-in pieces simmered in water with onion, celery, carrots, garlic, bay leaves, salt, and peppercorns. Simmer for at least an hour to build a rich broth. Remove the chicken, shred the meat, and discard the bones and vegetables used for stock. Strain the broth, then add fresh diced carrots, celery, and onion. Simmer until tender, add the shredded chicken back, season with salt and pepper, and finish with fresh parsley. Add egg noodles or rice if desired.

BANANA BREAD
Banana bread requires overripe bananas — the blacker the better, as they are sweeter and moister. You also need flour, sugar, butter, eggs, baking soda, salt, and vanilla. Mash the bananas thoroughly. Cream the butter and sugar, then mix in the eggs, mashed bananas, and vanilla. Stir in the dry ingredients: flour, baking soda, and salt. Do not overmix or the bread will be tough. Pour into a greased loaf pan and bake at 350°F (175°C) for 60 to 65 minutes until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean.

GUACAMOLE
Guacamole is made from ripe avocados, lime juice, salt, cilantro, diced white onion, jalapeño, and tomato. The avocados must be ripe — they should yield to gentle pressure. Mash the avocados with a fork, leaving some texture. Add lime juice immediately to prevent browning. Mix in finely diced onion, jalapeño, and tomato. Season with salt and stir in fresh cilantro. Taste and adjust lime and salt. Serve immediately or press plastic wrap directly against the surface to slow browning.

SCRAMBLED EGGS
Perfect scrambled eggs are made low and slow. Use two to three eggs per person, a knob of butter, and a pinch of salt. Whisk the eggs just until combined — do not overwhisk. Melt butter in a cold pan, then add the eggs and turn the heat to medium-low. Stir constantly with a spatula, scraping the bottom and sides. Remove the pan from heat regularly to control the temperature. The eggs should form soft, creamy curds. Remove from heat just before they look fully set as they will continue cooking. Season with salt at the end.

BEEF STEW
Beef stew requires chuck roast cut into cubes, potatoes, carrots, celery, onion, garlic, beef broth, tomato paste, Worcestershire sauce, flour, and herbs like thyme and rosemary. Brown the beef in batches in a hot Dutch oven — do not crowd the pan or the meat will steam instead of sear. Remove the beef and sauté the onion and garlic. Stir in tomato paste and flour, then add the beef back along with broth, Worcestershire, and herbs. Cover and cook at 325°F (165°C) for two hours. Add potatoes and carrots and cook another 45 minutes until everything is tender.

LEMON VINAIGRETTE
A simple lemon vinaigrette uses fresh lemon juice, olive oil, Dijon mustard, honey, garlic, salt, and black pepper. The ratio is one part lemon juice to three parts olive oil. Whisk together the lemon juice, Dijon mustard, honey, and minced garlic. Slowly drizzle in the olive oil while whisking constantly to emulsify. Season with salt and black pepper. This vinaigrette works well on green salads, roasted vegetables, and grilled fish.

PIZZA DOUGH
Basic pizza dough needs bread flour or all-purpose flour, active dry yeast, warm water, olive oil, salt, and a pinch of sugar. Dissolve the yeast in warm water with the sugar and let it sit for 5 minutes until foamy. Mix in flour, salt, and olive oil to form a dough. Knead for 8 to 10 minutes until smooth and elastic. Place in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise for one hour until doubled. Punch down and divide into portions. Let rest 15 minutes before stretching and topping.

FRENCH OMELETTE
A French omelette is smooth and pale yellow on the outside with no browning. You need three eggs, butter, salt, and your choice of filling such as cheese or herbs. Beat the eggs thoroughly with salt. Heat a small non-stick pan over medium-high heat and add butter. When the butter foams, pour in the eggs. Immediately stir in small circles with a spatula while shaking the pan. As the eggs begin to set, stop stirring and tilt the pan to roll the omelette toward one side. Flip it onto a plate seam-side down. It should be fully set outside but still creamy inside.
